# Art Deco Design Style

## Visual Characteristics

- **Geometric Precision**: Bold symmetrical patterns, chevrons, zigzags
- **Sunburst Motifs**: Radiating lines suggesting energy and optimism
- **Metallic Finishes**: Gold, chrome, brass, and silver accents
- **Rich Jewel Tones**: Emerald, sapphire, ruby, combined with black and gold
- **Streamlined Forms**: Sleek, aerodynamic shapes
- **Sans-Serif Typography with Flair**: Geometric letters with decorative touches
- **Angular Repetition**: Stepped patterns, fan shapes, parallelograms
- **Luxury Materials**: Marble, lacquer, exotic woods (in texture references)

## Origins & Evolution

Art Deco emerged in 1920s Paris, flourishing through the 1930s as a celebration of modernity, luxury, and machine-age optimism.

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1925 | Exposition Internationale des Arts Decoratifs in Paris names the style |
| 1927 | The Jazz Singer ushers in Art Deco Hollywood glamour |
| 1930 | Chrysler Building completed; quintessential Art Deco architecture |
| 1931 | Empire State Building opens |
| 1933 | Chicago World's Fair showcases Streamline Moderne |
| 1939 | New York World's Fair marks peak and transition |
| 1940s | WWII ends Art Deco's dominance |
| 1966 | Term "Art Deco" coined retroactively |
| 1974 | Art Deco revival begins with historic preservation movement |
| 2013 | The Great Gatsby film sparks major Art Deco revival |
| 2020s | Art Deco remains popular for luxury and event branding |

## Design Philosophy

### Core Principles

**Modernity as Luxury**
The machine age brings precision, speed, and elegance.

**Ordered Exuberance**
Celebration and glamour within geometric discipline.

**Democratic Luxury**
Mass production brings beauty to broader audiences.

**Optimistic Futurism**
Faith in progress and human achievement.

### Influences

- Ancient Egyptian motifs (following Tutankhamun discovery)
- Cubism and abstract art
- Machine aesthetics and industrial design
- African and Mesoamerican patterns
- Ballets Russes costumes and sets
- Bauhaus geometric principles

## Typography System

### Recommended Typefaces

| Role | Font | Weight | Size |
|------|------|--------|------|
| Display | Poiret One | 400 | 48-96px |
| Title | Josefin Sans | 400-600 | 28-48px |
| Heading | Bebas Neue | 400 | 20-36px |
| Body | Lato | 400 | 16-18px |
| Decorative | Broadway, Bifocals | 400 | Variable |

### Typography Guidelines

- **Geometric Letterforms**: Clean, precise sans-serifs
- **Letter-spacing**: Wide tracking for elegance
- **Case**: ALL CAPS common for headers
- **Weight**: Light to medium for elegance; bold for impact
- **Decoration**: Inline styles, outlines, shadowed effects
- **Numerals**: Often geometric, sometimes decorative

## UX Patterns

### Landing Page Hero Sections

**Pattern**: Dramatic, symmetrical hero areas with geometric framing

**Implementation**:
- Centered headlines with decorative frames
- Sunburst or radiating line backgrounds
- Gold accent borders and dividers
- Strong vertical symmetry

**Examples**: Event websites, luxury brand launches
**Best Practice**: Content must justify the grandeur

### Luxury E-commerce

**Pattern**: Premium product presentation with Art Deco elegance

**Implementation**:
- Black backgrounds with gold typography
- Geometric product framing
- Fan and chevron pattern accents
- Sophisticated hover states

**Examples**: High-end jewelry, watches, spirits
**Best Practice**: Elegance should enhance, not overwhelm products

### Event and Invitation Interfaces

**Pattern**: Digital invitations with Gatsby-era glamour

**Implementation**:
- Geometric border frames
- RSVP forms with period styling
- Countdown timers with Deco numerals
- Venue information in elegant layouts

**Examples**: Gala invitations, New Year's events
**Best Practice**: Forms must be usable despite decoration

### Menu and Pricing Displays

**Pattern**: Restaurant and service menus with period elegance

**Implementation**:
- Categories with decorative dividers
- Price lists in geometric alignment
- Descriptions with appropriate restraint
- Gold accents highlighting specials

**Examples**: Upscale restaurant websites, cocktail menus
**Best Practice**: Readability paramount; decoration supports hierarchy

### Loading and Transition States

**Pattern**: Art Deco animations for premium waiting experiences

**Implementation**:
- Geometric spinner patterns
- Radiating line animations
- Gold shimmer effects
- Fan unfold transitions

**Examples**: Luxury brand websites
**Best Practice**: Short, elegant; don't frustrate with extended animation
