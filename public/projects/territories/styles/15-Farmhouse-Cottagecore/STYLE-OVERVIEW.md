# Farmhouse / Cottagecore Design Style

## Visual Characteristics

- **Rustic Wood Textures**: Barn wood, shiplap, distressed planks
- **Natural Florals**: Wildflowers, dried lavender, simple blooms
- **Gingham and Plaid Patterns**: Classic country check patterns
- **Warm, Muted Colors**: Cream, sage, dusty blue, terracotta
- **Handwritten and Vintage Type**: Chalk-style, farmhouse serif fonts
- **Antique Finishes**: Galvanized metal, mason jars, vintage ceramics
- **Simple Botanical Illustrations**: Line drawings of herbs and plants
- **Cozy, Homespun Feel**: Emphasis on domestic crafts and comfort

## Origins & Evolution

Farmhouse style draws from American rural traditions, while cottagecore emerged as a romanticized internet aesthetic celebrating pastoral simplicity.

| Year | Milestone |
|------|-----------|
| 1800s | American farmhouse architecture establishes functional rural aesthetic |
| 1950s | "Country" decorating becomes distinct style category |
| 2000s | Modern farmhouse begins gaining traction in design media |
| 2013 | Fixer Upper TV show debuts, popularizing modern farmhouse |
| 2016 | Magnolia brand launches, codifying farmhouse aesthetic |
| 2018 | "Cottagecore" term emerges on Tumblr |
| 2020 | Pandemic accelerates cottagecore trend (escape to rural fantasy) |
| 2021 | Taylor Swift's "evermore" album aesthetics boost cottagecore |
| 2022 | Cottagecore influences gaming (Stardew Valley, Animal Crossing) |
| 2024 | Both styles remain popular for lifestyle and wellness branding |

## Design Philosophy

### Core Principles

**Simplicity and Authenticity**
Beauty in honest, functional objects made with care.

**Connection to Nature**
Rural landscapes, gardens, and seasonal rhythms as inspiration.

**Slow Living**
Rejecting hustle culture for deliberate, mindful domesticity.

**Nostalgia for Simpler Times**
Romanticizing pre-industrial craftsmanship and community.

### Influences

- American Shaker design philosophy
- English cottage garden tradition
- Scandinavian hygge concept
- Arts and Crafts movement
- Prairie and rural Americana
- Anne of Green Gables, Little House aesthetic

## Typography System

### Recommended Typefaces

| Role | Font | Weight | Size |
|------|------|--------|------|
| Display | Playfair Display | 700 | 48-72px |
| Title | Merriweather | 400 | 28-40px |
| Heading | Lora | 400 | 20-28px |
| Body | Source Serif Pro | 400 | 16-18px |
| Handwritten | Amatic SC, Caveat | 400 | Variable |

### Typography Guidelines

- **Serif Dominance**: Warm, readable serifs for most text
- **Handwritten Accents**: Chalk-style or script for emphasis
- **Weight**: Medium weights; nothing too heavy or light
- **Color**: Warm dark brown or charcoal, never pure black
- **Style**: Friendly, approachable, never corporate

## UX Patterns

### Recipe and Tutorial Interfaces

**Pattern**: Step-by-step guides for cooking, crafting, gardening

**Implementation**:
- Numbered steps with botanical dividers
- Ingredient lists styled as vintage cards
- Process photography with warm filters
- Print-friendly layouts

**Examples**: Pioneer Woman, Magnolia recipes
**Best Practice**: Clarity over decoration; recipes must be usable

### Lifestyle Blog Layouts

**Pattern**: Personal storytelling with pastoral photography

**Implementation**:
- Full-bleed hero images of rural scenes
- Long-form reading with generous margins
- Pull quotes with handwritten style
- Category navigation with botanical icons

**Examples**: Homestead blogs, lifestyle influencers
**Best Practice**: Fast loading despite rich imagery

### E-commerce Product Display

**Pattern**: Handmade and natural products presented with warmth

**Implementation**:
- Styled product photography on natural backgrounds
- Story sections explaining craft and origin
- Review displays with personal touch
- Bundle and collection presentations

**Examples**: Etsy shops, Magnolia Market
**Best Practice**: Don't let rustic styling hide purchase flow

### Seasonal and Calendar Interfaces

**Pattern**: Content organized by season, harvest, or natural cycles

**Implementation**:
- Season-specific color palettes
- Botanical illustrations for each period
- Calendar views with vintage aesthetic
- Weather and nature integrations

**Examples**: Gardening apps, seasonal recipe sites
**Best Practice**: Seasonal theming should enhance, not confuse

### Community and Gathering Features

**Pattern**: Forum and social features with cozy gathering metaphor

**Implementation**:
- "Porch" or "hearth" language for gathering spaces
- Warm member avatars and profiles
- Event features styled as invitations
- Sharing presented as "neighbor" recommendations

**Examples**: Homesteading forums, craft communities
**Best Practice**: Modern functionality with vintage appearance
